Where I attempt to understand the meaning of life and conquer the world.

Behold, ladies and gentlemen, running is good for the soul.

And yeah, that's basically it

In February, I reluctantly signed up for the Marion Arts Festival Half Marathon with my big sister. She somehow convinced me that running my first half marathon would be an extraordinary way to celebrate my high school graduation. The day before our monumental event, we purchased matching t-shirts that read 13.1, symbolizing both our motivation and desire.

On May 16th, three months after purchasing my ticket for the run, I felt as though I was underprepared. After dripping sweat for countless hours across my local gym’s treadmills and inside track, our run had arrived. The first mile was easy, as it always is. After embarking into the second mile, I felt a rush of pain throughout my body and a strong desire to quit. In that moment, the encouragement from other runners along the way inspired me to focus on my goal. Their cheers and support energized my spirit. After making it to mile five, I was ready to give in and bike my way to the finish. Biking is considerably easier than running. After 13.1 miles, my sister and I triumphantly raced to the finish line. The minded effort it took to complete this run paved my path to victory. We crossed together, courageously cheering each other on. My face was pale, my legs were jelly, and my heart was racing.

After crossing the finish line, the paleness of my face was identical to that of a ghost. Giving up was never an option, no matter how much I felt like dying. Yes, running is excessively difficult. Yes, running is imprudently demanding. But in that very moment, I knew I had done it. I had accomplished something others thought to be impossible. And to me, impossible is just a word. Its meaning has been morphed over time to represent something so far from its true connotation that its very essence is a desired outcome. Because of this, life is filled with so many different emotions, which, in turn, create memories. Moments like these create a lasting impact of possibilities.

There are possibilities in everything

The more in depth I thought about this belief, the more I agreed with its premise. In the twenty-first century, people are inclined to give plausible explanations on the successful, such as their knowledge, unique fortune, and other distinct qualities. No doubt, they will not reap achievement without these factors. However, the truth is that their success largely depends on the great efforts others hardly know. What's more, people are likely to claim their own failure is solely due to intelligence, which is superficial and ridiculous in my opinion. Let’s put it this way. For instance, the mathematical exam is on a scale of 100, and it’s really easy to achieve 60 points if you have right attitude on it. It takes your efforts to hit 80 points, and you really need to be very hard working if you would like to get 95 points. But, it somehow needs a bit gift to achieve 99 points. Actually, with 95 points, you have already been very competitive and it has nothing to do with your IQ. Personally, in most cases it never happens to beat IQ when competing with other people, but only the degree of diligence.

Achieving more

Normally, we consider people who are the talented because of their expertise on a specific field basically. Essentially, it is just because they have a good command of knowledge on that field. Without a little hard work, any given individual will have no emotional attachment into the game they are in. Whether you are a football player, a New York Times columnist, or a stay at home dad, every little task takes work. Most of those tasks, however, are done with minimal effort when the individual sees their strengths as talents. I also agree that becoming competitive is all too necessary when faced with a difficult task. Everything in life takes time. If you quit and give up on a dream you’ve had since a small child, all of your hard work and effort will be erased.

I feel that once you put in a certain amount of work, that task does not get easier - You just get better at it. As talent is a ‘natural aptitude or skill’, hard work simply remains a positive step in a sequence of events. Talent can be related to a skipping stone grazing the surface of a lake. The stone will eventually fall in but before it does, it bounces down a path. Many individuals with natural talent are able to skip a few steps. However, everyone eventually is faced with one question: Am I willing to work even harder to achieve something great?
